FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about the Zeph trainer and how it works.

Zeph uses clinical-grade flow and pressure sensors to measure key respiratory metrics including peak expiratory flow (PEF), forced vital capacity (FVC), and forced expiratory volume (FEV1). Simply breathe into the device as directed by the app, and the sensors capture precise data in real time.
The Zeph trainer features a 30-day rechargeable battery with typical daily use (two to three sessions per day). Charging is done via the included USB-C cable, and a full charge takes under 2 hours. A low-battery indicator in the app will notify you when it is time to recharge.
After each use, wipe the mouthpiece with a soft, damp cloth or an alcohol-free disinfecting wipe. The mouthpiece is detachable and can be rinsed under warm water. We recommend a thorough cleaning once a week and replacing the mouthpiece every 3 months.
The Zeph app is available on iOS 15+ and Android 10+ with Bluetooth 5.0 support. The app syncs seamlessly with the Zeph trainer via Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) for real-time data transfer.
